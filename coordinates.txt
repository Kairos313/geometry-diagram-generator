**DIMENSION: 3D**

### Part 1: Geometric Context from Question

#### 1. QUESTION OBJECTIVE
To determine the slant height of a square pyramid with base side length 6 cm and vertical height 8 cm, specifically the distance from the apex $P$ to the midpoint $M$ of one of the base edges.

#### 2. GIVEN ELEMENTS
*   **Given Lengths:** Base side length = 6 cm, Vertical height ($h$) = 8 cm.
*   **Given Properties:** Square pyramid; Apex $P$ is directly above the center of the base.
*   **Target:** Slant height $PM$, where $M$ is the midpoint of a base edge.

#### 3. ALL GEOMETRIC ELEMENTS
*   **Points:** 
    *   $O$: Center of the square base.
    *   $A, B, C, D$: Vertices of the square base.
    *   $P$: Apex of the pyramid.
    *   $M$: Midpoint of base edge $AD$.
*   **Lines:**
    *   Base edges: $AB, BC, CD, DA$.
    *   Lateral edges: $PA, PB, PC, PD$.
    *   Vertical height: $PO$.
    *   Slant height: $PM$.
    *   Apothem of base: $OM$.
*   **Faces:**
    *   Base: Square $ABCD$.
    *   Lateral faces: Triangles $PAB, PBC, PCD, PDA$.

### Part 2: Coordinate System and Scale Definition (Calculated)

#### 1. ORIGIN PLACEMENT
The origin $(0, 0, 0)$ is placed at the center of the square base ($O$).

#### 2. AXES ALIGNMENT
*   **X-axis:** Parallel to base edges $AB$ and $DC$.
*   **Y-axis:** Parallel to base edges $BC$ and $AD$.
*   **Z-axis:** Vertical, passing through the apex $P$.

#### 3. SCALE AND BOUNDS
*   **X-range:** [-4.000, 4.000]
*   **Y-range:** [-4.000, 4.000]
*   **Z-range:** [0.000, 9.000]
*   Scale: 1 unit = 1 cm.
