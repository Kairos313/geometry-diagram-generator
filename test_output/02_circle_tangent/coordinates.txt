## Geometric Blueprint

### Part 1: Geometric Context from Question

#### 1. QUESTION OBJECTIVE
A circle with center $O$ and radius $5$ cm is given. A point $P$ is located $13$ cm from the center. A tangent line from $P$ touches the circle at point $T$. The objective is to determine the length of the tangent segment $PT$.

#### 2. GIVEN ELEMENTS
*   **Given Lengths:** Radius $r = 5$ cm, $OP = 13$ cm.
*   **Given Properties:** $PT$ is tangent to the circle at $T$.
*   **Implied Property:** A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency ($\angle OTP = 90^\circ$).

#### 3. ALL GEOMETRIC ELEMENTS
*   **Points:** $O$ (center), $P$ (external point), $T$ (point of tangency).
*   **Lines/Segments:** $OT$ (radius), $OP$ (distance from center to point), $PT$ (tangent segment).
*   **Angles:** $\angle OTP$ (right angle).
*   **Polygons/Shapes:** Circle (center $O$, radius $5$ cm), $\triangle OTP$ (right-angled triangle).

### Part 2: Coordinate System and Scale Definition (Calculated)

#### 1. ORIGIN PLACEMENT (CRITICAL)
*   The center of the circle **O** is the origin: **(0.000, 0.000, 0.000)**.

#### 2. AXES ALIGNMENT
*   **Primary Axis (X-axis):** The vector from $O$ to $P$ defines the positive X-axis.
*   **Primary Plane (XY-plane):** The circle and point $P$ lie on the XY-plane ($Z = 0$).

#### 3. SCALE DEFINITION
*   **Method:** The distance $OP$ is the first significant distance. $OP = 13$ cm.
*   **Scale Reference:** $OP = 13$ cm = **5.000 units**.
*   **Scale Factor:** $5 / 13 \approx 0.3846$.
*   **Derived Scale Lengths:**
    *   Radius ($OT$) = $5$ cm $\times (5/13) \approx 1.923$ units.
    *   Tangent ($PT$) = $\sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = 12$ cm. In units: $12 \times (5/13) \approx 4.615$ units.
