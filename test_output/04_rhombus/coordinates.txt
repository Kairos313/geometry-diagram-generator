## Geometric Blueprint

### Part 1: Geometric Context from Question

#### 1. QUESTION OBJECTIVE
A rhombus $ABCD$ is defined by its diagonals $AC = 10$ cm and $BD = 24$ cm, which intersect at point $O$. The objective is to determine the side length of the rhombus.

#### 2. GIVEN ELEMENTS
*   **Given Lengths:** $AC = 10$ cm, $BD = 24$ cm.
*   **Given Properties:** $ABCD$ is a rhombus; diagonals $AC$ and $BD$ intersect at point $O$.
*   **Implied Properties:** In a rhombus, diagonals bisect each other at right angles ($90^\circ$). Thus, $AO = OC = 5$ cm, $BO = OD = 12$ cm, and $\angle AOB = 90^\circ$.

#### 3. ALL GEOMETRIC ELEMENTS
*   **Points:** $A, B, C, D, O$ (intersection of diagonals).
*   **Lines/Segments:** Sides $AB, BC, CD, DA$; diagonals $AC, BD$; half-diagonals $AO, OC, BO, OD$.
*   **Angles:** $\angle AOB, \angle BOC, \angle COD, \angle DOA$ (all $90^\circ$); interior angles $\angle DAB, \angle ABC, \angle BCD, \angle CDA$.
*   **Polygons:** Rhombus $ABCD$.

### Part 2: Coordinate System and Scale Definition (Calculated)

#### 1. ORIGIN PLACEMENT (CRITICAL)
*   The first vertex of the rhombus, **Point A**, is defined as the origin: **(0.000, 0.000, 0.000)**.

#### 2. AXES ALIGNMENT
*   **Primary Axis (X-axis):** The vector from $A$ to $C$ (the first mentioned diagonal) defines the positive X-axis.
*   **Primary Plane (XY-plane):** The rhombus $ABCD$ lies on the XY-plane ($Z = 0.000$).
*   **Vertical Axis (Z-axis):** Not applicable (2D problem).

#### 3. SCALE DEFINITION
*   **Method:** The first significant distance mentioned is $AC = 10$ cm. This is set to **5.0 units**.
*   **Scale Reference:** $AC = 10$ cm = $5.0$ units, scale factor = $5/10 = 0.5$.
*   All subsequent coordinates are calculated using this scale factor.
