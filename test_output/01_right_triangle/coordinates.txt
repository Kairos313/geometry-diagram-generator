=== GEOMETRIC BLUEPRINT - COORDINATES ===

## Geometric Blueprint

### Part 1: Geometric Context from Question

#### 1. QUESTION OBJECTIVE
*   Calculate the length of the hypotenuse $AB$ in a right-angled triangle $ABC$ where $\angle ACB = 90^\circ$, $AC = 6$ cm, and $BC = 8$ cm.

#### 2. GIVEN ELEMENTS
*   **Given Lengths:** $AC = 6$ cm, $BC = 8$ cm.
*   **Given Angles:** $\angle ACB = 90^\circ$.
*   **Given Properties:** Triangle $ABC$ is a right triangle with the right angle at $C$.

#### 3. ALL GEOMETRIC ELEMENTS
*   **Points:** $A, B, C$.
*   **Lines/Segments:** $AC$ (leg), $BC$ (leg), $AB$ (hypotenuse).
*   **Angles:** $\angle ACB$ (right angle), $\angle BAC$, $\angle ABC$.
*   **Polygons/Shapes:** Triangle $ABC$.

### Part 2: Coordinate System and Scale Definition (Calculated)

#### 1. ORIGIN PLACEMENT (CRITICAL)
*   Point **A** is chosen as the origin: **(0.000, 0.000, 0.000)**.

#### 2. AXES ALIGNMENT
*   **Primary Axis (X-axis):** The vector from $A$ to $C$ defines the positive X-axis.
*   **Primary Plane (XY-plane):** The triangle $ABC$ lies on the XY-plane ($Z = 0$).
*   **Vertical Axis (Z-axis):** Not applicable (2D problem).

#### 3. SCALE DEFINITION
*   **Method:** The first significant distance mentioned is $AC = 6$ cm. Set $AC = 5.0$ units.
*   **Scale Reference:** $AC = 6$ cm = $5.0$ units. Scale factor = $5/6 \approx 0.8333$.
*   **Subsequent Lengths:**
    *   $BC = 8$ cm $\times (5/6) = 6.667$ units.
    *   $AB = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = 10$ cm. $10$ cm $\times (5/6) = 8.333$ units.
